The Ruling on Celebrating Christmas in Islam

What is the ruling on celebrating Christmas in Islam?

Celebrating Christmas is prohibited in Islam because it involves imitating non-Muslims and goes against Islamic beliefs. Muslims should not dedicate Christmas days to any type of celebration, decoration, or food, as this would be considered participation in non-Muslim holidays, which is unquestionably forbidden.

The Christmas Tree and Its Ruling in Islam?

The Christmas tree is one of the symbols of Christian holidays, and it is prohibited in Islam to possess it because it represents imitation of non-Muslims and glorification of their rituals. The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) said: "Whoever imitates a people is one of them." Therefore, a Muslim should not place a Christmas tree in their home, even if they do not celebrate it, as this is considered a prohibited form of imitation or glorification of a specific religious symbol.
